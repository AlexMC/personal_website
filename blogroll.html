Blogroll

Blogs (RSS feeds) I Follow

I mostly use RSS feeds to follow blogs and websites, so that's what I'm sharing here. My RSS reader of choince both on Mac and iOS is Reeder.

I also read my favorite newsletters trough my RSS feed using Kill the Newsletter!

Technology

  • By far my main source of tech related news is Hacker News. I use the RSS feeds from https://hnrss.github.io/ mainly:

    • Active
    • Front Page
    • Newest
    • Best
    • Best Comments

  • Simon Willison’s Weblog a great source of news and insights on everything related with AI development.

  • The Rundown AI My favorite daily newsletter on everything AI.

  • The Neuron My second favorite daily newsletter on everything AI.

  • Daring Fireball Insights into the Apple ecosystem.

  • Tomasz Tunguz Tomasz is a tech VC who writes very well and always offers great, data-driven insights into what’s happening in tech.

  • Spotify Engineering Always fascinated with their technical posts.

  • One Useful Thing Prof. Ethan Mollik always writes very thoughtfull articles on the Implications of AI.

  • MacStories Federico Viticci, John Voorhees, and the rest of the MacStories crew always have the best content on the Apple ecosystem.

Gamming

I have always been fascinated by the gaming world. Nowadays, I don’t play much, but I still enjoy keeping up with what’s happening and often find inspiration in this world for my day-to-day work.

  • Wavelengths Brandon is a game aficionado and a great writer.
  • Polygon A generalist gaming site.
  • Metal Game Solid Metal Game Solid is a recent website that focuses on retro gaming news (a sub-genre I'm really fond of)

Lego

I'm what nowadays is called an AFOL (Adult Fan of LEGO)...

  • The Brothers Brick Probably the best blog covering the Adult LEGO fans world.
  • Brickset Primarly a database of LEGO sets they are also very fast at reporting on LEGo related news.

Other

  • News Minimalist An interesting experiment, they crawl 30K plus news articles per day and give them a significance score, surfacing daily the handful that are most significant.
  • Dense Discovery I don't know how to describe this newsletter but I know I love it. Maybe the best way is to quote Kai, the author:

I now send a weekly newsletter that curates a mix of the practical and contemplative, highlighting paths toward a more considered existence online and in real life – one that resists the pull of the attention merchants.
